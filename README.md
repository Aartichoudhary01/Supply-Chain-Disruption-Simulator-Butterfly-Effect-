# Supply-Chain-Disruption-Simulator-Butterfly-Effect-
An interactive Supply Chain Simulator dashboard that analyzes sales, shipping, customer behavior, and regional performance to uncover business insights. It also simulates real-world disruptions to help predict risks and enable data-driven decision-making.
## 1. Project Objective

The objective of this project is to build an interactive Supply Chain Simulator – “Butterfly Effect” Dashboard that analyzes how small disruptions in logistics and operations impact overall business performance.

### The project aims to:

- Monitor sales, profit, and shipping performance
- Analyze customer and product behavior
- Identify regional inefficiencies
- Simulate real-world disruptions (e.g., Trade War, Oil Price Collapse)
- Enable data-driven decision-making for supply chain optimization
## 2. Dataset Used

The dataset used in this project is a global supply chain dataset containing detailed transactional and logistics information, including:

Order Details: Order ID, Order Date, Shipping Date
Product Information: Product Name, Category, Quantity
Customer Data: Customer Segment, Region, Market
Sales Metrics: Sales, Profit, Revenue
Shipping Details: Shipping Mode, Delivery Status, Delay
Geographical Data: Country, State, Region
Simulated Disruptions: Brexit Shock, Hurricane Harvey, Trade War, Oil Price Collapse

This dataset enables both historical analysis and simulation-based forecasting.

## 3. Questions / KPIs

The dashboards are designed to answer key business questions through important KPIs:

## Key Questions
- Which regions and markets generate the highest sales?
- How do shipping delays affect sales and profit?
- Which products and categories perform the best?
- How do customer segments contribute to revenue?
- What is the impact of disruptions on business performance?
- Which regions are most vulnerable to delays and risks?
## Key KPIs
- Total Sales
- Total Profit
- Total Orders
- Average Shipping Days
- Late Delivery Risk
- Sales Impact %
- Profit Impact %
- Order Status Distribution
- Category-wise Sales
- Market-wise Sales

## 4. Process

The project follows a structured data analytics pipeline:

1. Data Collection
Imported supply chain dataset into Power BI
2. Data Cleaning & Preparation
Removed inconsistencies and null values
Standardized columns (dates, regions, categories)
Created calculated fields (e.g., delay, profit impact)
3. Data Modeling
Established relationships between tables
Created measures using DAX (Sales, Profit, Delay, KPIs)
4. Dashboard Development

Built 6 interactive dashboards:

* Overview Dashboard – Overall performance summary
* Shipping Analysis – Delivery performance & delays
* Sales & Profit Impact – Effect of disruptions
* Customer & Product Impact – Customer behavior & product insights
* Regional Analysis – Geographic performance
* Supply Chain Simulator – Scenario-based analysis
5. Visualization
Used bar charts, line charts, maps, scatter plots, KPI cards
Applied filters for dynamic exploration
## 5. Project Insights
  ## Sales & Market Insights
- Europe is the top-performing market, contributing the highest revenue
- Sales are unevenly distributed, indicating dependency on specific regions
- Shipping & Logistics Insights
- Average shipping time is around 3.5 days
- Certain regions and states show higher delivery delays, indicating inefficiencies
- Standard shipping has longer delays compared to faster modes
  ## Customer Insights
- The Consumer segment contributes over 50% of total sales
- Customer behavior is highly influenced by delivery performance
  ## Product Insights
- A few products dominate sales, indicating high dependency on top items
- Some categories are more sensitive to delays and disruptions
  ## Disruption Insights
- External factors like Trade War, Oil Price Collapse, and Natural Disasters impact:
- Sales trends
- Profit margins
- Delivery efficiency
- Simulations help predict potential business risks
- Delay vs Profit Relationship
- Increased shipping delays can negatively impact profit
However, high-demand products may remain profitable despite delays
